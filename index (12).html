The effects

Using your mobile phone is a distraction. It impairs:
  • Reaction time Multitasking when driving slows down reaction time, which can be the difference between swerving in time to avoid a pedestrian or causing a deadly accident
  • Ability to judge distances between cars and other objects
  • Maintaining correct road positions and the required speed limits
  • Awareness of other road users
  • Visual search patterns. It is hard to assess the environmental conditions and decide where to turn or what distances to keep when half the focus is on a phoneâ€™s screen.
  • Hinders concentration. Checking a handset while driving hinders concentration, which is why using a mobile phone when driving is so dangerous.
